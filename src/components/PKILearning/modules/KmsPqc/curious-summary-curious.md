Large enterprises use millions of digital 'keys' to lock and unlock their data constantly. Managing all of these keys is handled by a central, highly secure brain called a Key Management System (KMS).

To rapidly prepare for the quantum computing threat, this entire central brain must be fully upgraded. It needs to securely generate and flawlessly track completely new, mathematically heavy Post-Quantum keys alongside the older ones. Upgrading a KMS guarantees that a sprawling company won't lose absolute control of its massive digital key ring during the transition.
