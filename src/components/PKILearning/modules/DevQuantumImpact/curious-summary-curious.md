The new quantum-proof encryption is entirely different from what programmers are used to. These new math locks are physically massive—sometimes 50 times larger than the old ones.

Because programmers assumed digital locks would always be microscopic, modern apps and networks were built with strict file-size limits. Trying to jam a massive new Post-Quantum lock into a tiny, hardcoded app limit will instantly crash the app, drop messages, and overwhelm the system's memory.

To prepare for the quantum transition, developers must audit their code immediately. They need to find all the places where tight size limits were carelessly programmed, and redesign those boundaries so that their applications can smoothly handle the massive weight of the new Post-Quantum math.
