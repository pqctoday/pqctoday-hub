# Quantum Threats — In Simple Terms

## What This Is About
Modern encryption relies on math problems (like factoring massive numbers) that are practically impossible for traditional computers to solve. A Cryptographically Relevant Quantum Computer (CRQC) running "Shor's Algorithm" changes everything—it can instantly crack the RSA and ECC encryption protecting the internet.

## Why It Matters
Hostile nation-states and heavily funded syndicates are performing "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" operations today. They are actively intercepting and hoarding encrypted intellectual property, state secrets, and financial data now, patiently waiting to decrypt it the moment a powerful quantum computer turns on.

## The Key Takeaway
We cannot wait for quantum computers to be built before reacting. Organizations must immediately identify vulnerable data and migrate to NIST's new post-quantum algorithms (FIPS 203, 204, 205) well before CRQCs come online.

## What's Happening
Intelligence agencies worldwide have issued urgent mandates for the transition, establishing aggressive deadlines to rip-and-replace vulnerable cryptographic systems to safeguard long-term national security assets.
