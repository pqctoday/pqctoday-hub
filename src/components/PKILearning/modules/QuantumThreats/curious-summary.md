Curious about why quantum computers are considered a threat?

**The Problem:** A theoretical "quantum computer" works fundamentally differently than standard computers, using computational physics to easily bypass the mathematical locks that secure the internet today.
**The Solution:** The entire world must transition to new mathematical locks that rely on completely different, much harder mathematical problems (known as Post-Quantum Cryptography).
**The Strategy:** Because hackers intercept encrypted data today to decrypt it later, organizations cannot afford to wait; they must adopt these new locks immediately for their most sensitive, long-lasting data.
**The Ecosystem:** This effort requires collaboration between cryptographers, intelligence agencies, enterprise security leaders, and software vendors.
