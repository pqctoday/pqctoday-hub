Artificial Intelligence learns from massive amounts of data, acting like an engine fueled by information. We protect this valuable data by locking it up with secret codes (encryption) to keep it safe from prying eyes.

However, a new kind of super-powerful machine called a "quantum computer" is being built. These machines will eventually be strong enough to pick the traditional locks we use today. This means hackers who steal our encrypted data right now might be able to read it in the future once they get their hands on a quantum computer.

To prevent this, security experts are rolling out "Post-Quantum Cryptography" (PQC). This is essentially an upgrade to a brand new type of incredibly complex mathematical lock that even future quantum computers won't be able to break. Upgrading our systems to PQC ensures that AI models stay secure, their data remains private, and their actions can always be trusted.
