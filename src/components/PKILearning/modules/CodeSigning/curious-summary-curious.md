Whenever you download an app or a software update, your computer checks a hidden digital signature. Think of this signature like an unbreakable wax seal from the software creator (like Microsoft or Apple). If the seal is unbroken, your computer knows the update is safe to install.

However, a future quantum computer could easily forge these digital seals. This means a hacker could create a highly destructive virus and stamp it with a perfectly forged "Apple" or "Microsoft" wax seal. Millions of computers would blindly accept and install the fake update, believing it was from a trusted source.

To prevent this, the software industry is upgrading to Post-Quantum digital seals. These new seals use completely different, massive mathematical equations that a quantum computer cannot forge. This ensures that when you download an update in the future, you can still guarantee exactly who created it.
