The massive 5G cellular networks powering millions of smartphones and connected vehicles rely on incredibly complex webs of digital trust between cell towers, devices, and the internet core.

Because a quantum computer could effortlessly shatter the digital locks protecting these high-speed connections, attackers could theoretically instantly reroute or intercept massive amounts of global cellular traffic. Telecommunications giants are actively baking completely new Post-Quantum math into the absolute core design of next-generation 5G networks to eliminate this threat before it materializes.
