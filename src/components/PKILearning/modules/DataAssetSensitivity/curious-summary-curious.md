Right now, bad actors are sucking up and storing massive amounts of encrypted data from the internet. They can't read it today, but they are hoarding it to unlock it later when a powerful quantum computer is built. This means any sensitive information that needs to stay private for decades is already at risk.

You can't upgrade every single piece of data to quantum-proof security overnight. It takes time and money. To decide what to tackle first, a company must sort all its data into tiers—like Low, Medium, High, and Critical importance.

By classifying data according to how sensitive it is and how long it legally needs to be kept private, a company can prioritize their efforts. They can urgently apply the new, unbreakable Post-Quantum locks to the "Critical" data first, ensuring their most valuable secrets are shielded before a quantum computer arrives.
